## Common Patterns

There are two recurring loser archetypes.

### Pattern 1: Weak Market Breadth / Narrow Market

Examples:

- AMBUJACEM
- TECHM
- AUBANK
- BRITANNIA
- DRREDDY

The stock may pass its own momentum filters, but the broader market has poor participation. These trades have higher failure risk because the move is not broadly supported.

This pattern is real but moderate:

- Weak market breadth loser rate: 47.22%
- Non-weak-market loser rate: 42.24%

That is enough for a sizing overlay experiment, not enough for a hard skip.

### Pattern 2: Late Momentum / Crowded Extension

Examples:

- THERMAX
- ADANIPORTS
- ELGIEQUIP
- GODREJIND
- AAVAS

These trades had strong ADX, decent or high RSI, and price well above EMA200. They looked strong at entry, but the subsequent 20-day window captured mean reversion instead of continuation.

This is why simple Fibonacci cuts failed: many winners also pull back through these same levels before continuing.

## What Is Not The Main Pattern

1. **Weak ADX is not the problem**
   - The system already selects high-ADX names.
   - Big losers generally had strong ADX.

2. **VWAP entry quality is not the problem**
   - The final 2.5% previous-day VWAP skip already removed the worst stretched entries.
   - In the biggest 30 losers, only a small number entered meaningfully above VWAP.

3. **EMA200 overextension alone is not enough**
   - Some big losers were extended, but overextended trades as a group were still profitable.

4. **Sector breadth alone is not enough**
   - Worst 30 losers include 16 high-sector-breadth cases.
   - Sector strength can be real, but individual names can still reverse sharply.

## Conclusion

The biggest losers do not share one clean mechanical failure.

The strongest common theme is:

```text
Good-looking stock momentum inside an imperfect market backdrop.
```

The most useful risk idea is not a hard exit or skip. It is likely a **position sizing overlay**:

- Full size when market breadth is healthy.
- Half size when market breadth is weak or when the stock is both extended and market breadth is weak.
- Avoid changing the entry model until this is tested as a portfolio-level experiment.
